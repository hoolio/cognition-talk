# Critical memo

**The insight becomes an investment heuristic: look for companies that either (1) own the new system layer where expertise is being encoded, (2) preserve interpretability/control as cognition is black-boxed, or (3) verticalize cognition where judgment is expensive, regulated, and workflow-bound. Avoid undifferentiated companies merely wrapping frontier models around generic knowledge work.**

## 1. The thesis

AI is not simply automating white-collar work. It is **industrializing cognition**.

The old transition was:

> craft → profession → industrial system → commodity

The AI transition is:

> tacit expert judgment → model-mediated workflow → system of record / system of action → commoditized output

The investable question is:

> **Where does value accrue when reasoning becomes cheap?**

Not “who uses AI?” Everyone will.

The better question:

> **Who owns the layer where formerly scarce expertise becomes reusable infrastructure?**

## 2. The matrix

Score companies across seven factors:

| Factor | Question | High score means |
|----|----|----|
| **Cognitive Externalization** | Does the product encode expert work into repeatable systems? | It turns tacit judgment into software |
| **Workflow Ownership** | Does it sit inside recurring work, not one-off generation? | It becomes habitual infrastructure |
| **Epistemic Trust / Auditability** | Can users inspect, verify, cite, trace, or govern outputs? | It solves black-box risk |
| **System-of-Action Potential** | Can it execute, not just recommend? | It captures transaction/workflow value |
| **Data / Feedback Loop** | Does usage improve the product or create proprietary training data? | Compounding advantage |
| **Commoditization Resistance** | Is it hard for OpenAI/Anthropic/Microsoft/Google to crush? | Durable wedge |
| **Budget Gravity** | Does it attach to large existing spend? | Easy path to revenue scale |

The highest-quality companies are not necessarily “most AI.” They are companies where AI **moves expertise into infrastructure** and captures the resulting control point.

## 3. The core categories

### A. Foundation model labs

**Examples:** OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI, Mistral, Cohere, Perplexity, DeepSeek, Reka, Reflection, Thinking Machines.

These are the new **industrial foundries of cognition**. They encode general reasoning capacity and sell it as infrastructure. OpenAI, Anthropic, and Databricks were among the biggest valuation movers in the 2025 unicorn market; PwC reported AI represented 43% of Top 100 unicorn value in 2025, with OpenAI rising to \$300B, Anthropic to \$183B, and Databricks to \$100B in that report’s period.

**Heuristic score:**\
Cognitive externalization: 5\
Workflow ownership: 3\
Trust/auditability: 2–3\
System-of-action: 3\
Feedback loop: 5\
Commoditization resistance: 5 if frontier; 2 if subscale\
Budget gravity: 5

**VC read:** massive value capture, but brutal capital intensity. These companies are Zeiss + Bell Labs + electric utilities.

### B. AI infrastructure / inference / compute

**Examples:** CoreWeave, Lambda, Together AI, Fireworks, Baseten, Groq, Cerebras, Celestial AI, Modular, TensorWave, Crusoe, Foundry, Vast Data, Weka, RunPod.

This layer profits from the industrialization of cognition without needing to own end-user judgment. TechCrunch tracked numerous 2025 mega-rounds in this infrastructure bucket, including Fireworks, Cerebras, Groq, Baseten, Together AI, Lambda, Celestial AI, Modular, TensorWave, and others.

**Heuristic score:**\
Cognitive externalization: 3\
Workflow ownership: 3\
Trust/auditability: 2\
System-of-action: 2\
Feedback loop: 3\
Commoditization resistance: 3–5 depending on hardware/supply constraints\
Budget gravity: 5

**VC read:** picks-and-shovels. Strong if capacity, latency, cost, or deployment constraints are real. Weak if it becomes margin-compressed cloud resale.

### C. Agent infrastructure / browser and web agents

**Examples:** Parallel, Adept, MultiOn, Browserbase, Tavily, Exa, Replit Agent infra, LangChain, LlamaIndex, Dust, CrewAI, Lindy.

This is where “reasoning” becomes **action over external systems**. Parallel, for example, is explicitly building web infrastructure for AI agents and recently raised at a reported \$2B valuation.

**Heuristic score:**\
Cognitive externalization: 4\
Workflow ownership: 4\
Trust/auditability: 3\
System-of-action: 5\
Feedback loop: 4\
Commoditization resistance: 3–4\
Budget gravity: 4

**VC read:** very important category. The risk is that browsers, OS vendors, and model labs absorb it. The opportunity is becoming the “Visa network” or “AWS Lambda” for agentic work.

This is probably the cleanest expression of the thesis. These companies take expensive professional judgment and make it workflow-native. Harvey and EvenUp in legal, Abridge and OpenEvidence in healthcare, Sierra in customer service, and Hippocratic in healthcare agents all raised large 2025 rounds according to TechCrunch.

Feedback loop: 4\

**VC read:** best near-term commercial category. The winners will not be “chatbots for lawyers/doctors/support.” They will be systems that own documents, workflows, approvals, liability boundaries, and institutional memory.

## 6. The clean VC heuristic

The best company is not the one that “uses AI.”

The best company is one where:

> **AI captures an expensive cognitive workflow, embeds itself in the system of record or system of action, compounds through proprietary feedback, and preserves enough trust that institutions can rely on it.**

The weak company is:

> **a prompt interface over a generic model producing substitutable outputs.**

The strongest wedge is:

> **industrialized cognition with inspectability.**

That is the rare combination: automation plus epistemic control.

For Observable, that points directly at the white space: not “AI data analysis,” but **the inspectable runtime for cognitive work**—a place where agents can reason, execute, show intermediate state, produce durable artifacts, and avoid becoming an opaque black box.
